A realistic physics simulation of a car braking on a wet road.

A modern sedan is driving on a rainy asphalt road at constant speed.
The driver suddenly applies emergency brakes.

The tires generate friction force against the wet surface.
The wheels slow down first, then the whole vehicle gradually decelerates because of inertia.

Water sprays from the tires.
The car slides slightly before coming to a complete stop.

The braking distance depends on friction between tires and road.
The motion follows Newtonian mechanics.
